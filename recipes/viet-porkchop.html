VIETNAMESE PORK CHOPS!

Vietnamese Pork Chop Recipe

Vietnamese pork chop, also known as "suon nuong" in Vietnamese, is a popular and flavorful dish in Vietnamese cuisine. It typically consists of marinated pork chops that are grilled or pan-fried to perfection. The pork chops are marinated in a mixture of fish sauce, garlic, lemongrass, sugar, and other seasonings, which infuses them with a delicious blend of sweet, savory, and aromatic flavors. The result is tender and juicy pork chops with a caramelized and slightly charred exterior. Vietnamese pork chops are often served with steamed rice, pickled vegetables, and a side of fish sauce-based dipping sauce, creating a balanced and satisfying meal.

In summary, Vietnamese pork chop is a mouthwatering dish featuring marinated and grilled or pan-fried pork chops. The marinade, composed of fish sauce, garlic, lemongrass, and other seasonings, imparts a delightful combination of sweet, savory, and aromatic flavors to the meat. Served with steamed rice, pickled vegetables, and a dipping sauce, Vietnamese pork chops offer a harmonious and fulfilling dining experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs porkchops(about 6 chops)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1/2 tsp MSG
  • 4 tbsp neutral cooking oil
  • 6 tbsp lemongrass, minced
  • 2 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp shallots, minced
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 3 tbsp scallion & oil
  • 3 tbsp dipping fish sauce

Steps

  1. Rinse pork chops under running water and lightly scrub with salt. Pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Combine all marinade ingredients in a medium mixing bowl and stir to combine and dissolve sugar and MSG. Then add the Marinade Aromatics.
  3. Marinade the pork chops in a bowl or bag in the fridge for at least 3 hours, but ideally overnight.
  4. Remove the chops from the marinade and discard the extra liquid–you can keep the aromatics on that stuck to the chops though.
  5. Pan fry: 1-2 chops at a time on medium to medium high heat without crowding the pan. When done, the pork should read 145 °F using an instant read thermometer at the thickest part of the cut.
